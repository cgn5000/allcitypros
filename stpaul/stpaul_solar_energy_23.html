Frequently Asked Questions

How much do solar panels cost in St. Paul?
A typical residential solar installation in St. Paul costs $15,000–$25,000 before incentives. After the 30% federal tax credit (ITC), the net cost drops to $10,500–$17,500. Most St. Paul homeowners break even in 6–10 years and save $20,000–$40,000 over the system's lifetime.
What solar incentives are available in MN?
MN homeowners can claim the 30% federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) on the full cost of their solar system. MN may also offer additional state tax credits, net metering policies, and utility rebates. A St. Paul solar installer can identify every incentive you qualify for.
How much can solar reduce my electricity bill in St. Paul?
Most St. Paul homeowners eliminate 70–100% of their electricity bill with a properly sized solar system. Your exact savings depend on your current usage, roof size and orientation, and local utility rates. A St. Paul solar company will model your savings before you sign anything.
Do solar panels work on cloudy days?
Yes — solar panels generate electricity from daylight, not direct sun. They're less efficient on cloudy days (typically 10–25% of peak output) but still produce meaningful energy. St. Paul homeowners in cloudier climates still achieve excellent payback through net metering.
How long do solar panels last?
Quality solar panels carry 25-year performance warranties and often last 30–40 years. System output degrades slowly — about 0.5% per year. Your St. Paul solar installer will show you a production model projecting output over the full warranty period.
