Frequently Asked Questions

How much does HVAC repair cost in Overland Park?
HVAC repair costs in Overland Park typically range from $150–$500 for minor fixes like a capacitor or refrigerant recharge, up to $1,500+ for compressor or coil replacement. Get 2–3 free estimates from local Overland Park HVAC companies before committing.
How often should I service my HVAC system?
Most manufacturers recommend a professional tune-up twice a year — spring for your AC and fall for your heating system. Annual maintenance extends equipment life, improves efficiency, and catches small issues before they become expensive breakdowns.
What SEER rating should I look for?
SEER (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio) measures cooling efficiency. Modern systems run 14–25 SEER. In warmer climates a 16–18 SEER unit offers the best balance of upfront cost and long-term energy savings.
How long do HVAC systems last?
Central AC units last 15–20 years and furnaces 20–30 years with proper maintenance. If your system is over 15 years old and needs a major repair, replacement is often more cost-effective. A local Overland Park HVAC technician can assess your system during a free inspection.
Do Overland Park HVAC companies offer emergency same-day service?
Yes — most Overland Park HVAC companies provide 24/7 emergency service for situations like a broken AC in summer heat or a failed furnace in winter. Many charge no after-hours surcharge for genuine emergencies.
