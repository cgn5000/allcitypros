Frequently Asked Questions

How much do solar panels cost in Sioux Falls?
A typical residential solar installation in Sioux Falls costs $15,000–$25,000 before incentives. After the 30% federal tax credit (ITC), the net cost drops to $10,500–$17,500. Most Sioux Falls homeowners break even in 6–10 years and save $20,000–$40,000 over the system's lifetime.
What solar incentives are available in SD?
SD homeowners can claim the 30% federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) on the full cost of their solar system. SD may also offer additional state tax credits, net metering policies, and utility rebates. A Sioux Falls solar installer can identify every incentive you qualify for.
How much can solar reduce my electricity bill in Sioux Falls?
Most Sioux Falls homeowners eliminate 70–100% of their electricity bill with a properly sized solar system. Your exact savings depend on your current usage, roof size and orientation, and local utility rates. A Sioux Falls solar company will model your savings before you sign anything.
Do solar panels work on cloudy days?
Yes — solar panels generate electricity from daylight, not direct sun. They're less efficient on cloudy days (typically 10–25% of peak output) but still produce meaningful energy. Sioux Falls homeowners in cloudier climates still achieve excellent payback through net metering.
How long do solar panels last?
Quality solar panels carry 25-year performance warranties and often last 30–40 years. System output degrades slowly — about 0.5% per year. Your Sioux Falls solar installer will show you a production model projecting output over the full warranty period.
