Frequently Asked Questions

How much does roof replacement cost in Mesa?
A full roof replacement in Mesa typically costs $8,000–$18,000 for an average-sized home with asphalt shingles, depending on roof size, pitch, and materials. Metal and tile roofs run higher. Get 2–3 free estimates from licensed Mesa roofing contractors.
How long does a roof last?
Asphalt shingles: 20–30 years. Architectural shingles: 25–30 years. Metal roofing: 40–70 years. Tile: 50+ years. Regular inspections by a Mesa roofing contractor can catch minor issues and extend your roof's life significantly.
How do I know if my roof needs replacement vs repair?
Signs you may need replacement: shingles curling, cracking, or missing in large areas; granules in gutters; daylight through attic; roof age over 20 years; repeated leaks after repairs. A licensed Mesa roofer can assess during a free inspection.
Does homeowner's insurance cover roof replacement in Mesa?
Most AZ homeowner's policies cover storm-related damage (wind, hail) but not normal wear and tear. After a severe storm, have a licensed Mesa roofing contractor document the damage before you file a claim — they can often assist with the insurance process.
How long does a roof replacement take?
Most residential roof replacements in Mesa are completed in 1–2 days by an experienced crew. Larger or more complex roofs may take 2–3 days. Your Mesa roofer should give you a clear timeline and communicate any weather-related delays.
